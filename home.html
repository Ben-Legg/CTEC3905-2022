So Why Houseplants, What's the Point?

The Journal of Physiological Anthorpology conducted a study which concluded that bringing plants into your home environment can reduce stress.

Indoor plants are believed to increase your attention span which can lead to a boost in productivity in working environments, such as at work and school.

Research has also found that working with plants can be therapeutic and help you recover from illness faster.

Since a NASA study in the 1980s, multiple studies have suggested that plants remove contaminants from indoor air, which can improve the quality of the air we breath.

Popular Types of Houseplants

TRAILING & CLIMBING PLANTS

Despite needing lots of attention, if cared for properly, plants that trail and climb can bring much joy to a plain room.

INDOOR PALMS

Being some of the easiest to care for, indoor palms are favoured due to their large sizes and low maintenance levels.

CACTI

Like palms, cacti require infrequent watering and need very little upkeep. If given enough time and sun, cacti also produce flowers in the spring months.

HANGING PLANTS

Perfect for homes short of space, hanging plants enjoy lots of sun and produce plenty of foliage to add colour to you home.
